aidWare Speech & Literacy Screener

Identify early risk factors for 2;0 - 3;11 year olds

Child Information
Communication Checklist (2;0–3;11)

1. Prelinguistic Communication

Item Yes No
I. My child waves "bye-bye" or claps
II. My child points to show interest or request for something
III. My child shakes head for "no"
IV. My child reaches toward desired objects
V. My child uses gestures alongside sounds or words.

2. Understanding (Receptive Language)

Item Yes No
I. My child responds to their name when called
II. My child follows simple directions like “come here” or “give me”.
III. My child understands common words like "ball", "milk", "mommy".
IV. My child looks at the correct object when asked "Where's the dog?".
V. My child follows simple directions like “come here” or “give me”.

3. Speaking (Expressive Language)

Item Yes No
I. My child babbles with varied sounds (e.g., “ba-da”, “ma-ma”).
II. My child uses at least 10 meaningful words.
III. My child combines two words like “more juice” or “mommy go”.
IV. My child names familiar people or objects (e.g., “daddy”, “car”).
V. My child follows imitates words or sounds heard in the environment.

4. Social Interaction (Pragmatic Language)

Item Yes No
I. My child smiles or laughs during social games (e.g., peekaboo).
II. My child looks at you or others when talking or playing.
III. My child initiates communication (e.g., brings a toy to show).
IV. My child takes turns during simple games.
V. My child shows awareness of other people’s feelings (e.g., comfort someone who is upset).

5. Symbolic Play

Item Yes No
I. My child pretends to feed a doll or stuffed animal.
II. My child uses objects for pretend actions (e.g., uses a block as a phone).
III. My child imitates adult routines (e.g., sweeping, talking on phone).
IV. My child acts out stories or familiar routines with toys..
V. My child combines toys in play (e.g., puts a figure into a toy car).

6. Emergent Literacy

Item Yes No
I. My child enjoys looking at books with others
II. My child points to and names pictures in books.
III. My child imitates reading or pretends to read.
IV. My child recognizes familiar signs or logos (e.g., cereal boxes).
V. My child tries to sing along with rhymes or songs.

7. General Development

Item Yes No
I. My child turns toward sounds or voices and responds to name being called (hearing).
II. My child makes eye contact and visually tracks moving objects or people (vision).
III. My child uses arms and legs to move around (e.g., crawling, cruising, walking).
IV. My child picks up small objects using thumb and finger (fine motor/pincer grasp).
V. My child uses both hands together during play (e.g., holding a toy and banging it).
VI. My child shows interest in people or interacts during simple games (e.g., peekaboo).
VII. My child calms down with comfort or routine when upset (emotional regulation).
VIII. My child climbs onto furniture or stairs with or without help (gross motor).
IX. My child imitates simple everyday actions (e.g., brushing hair, stirring)..
X. My child eats all age appropriate foods without difficulties (e.g., no coughing, no regurgitation).
