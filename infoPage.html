The Silent Crisis in Early Childhood

1 in 6

children under six has a developmental delay, yet the vast majority are not identified until after they start school.

For children in underserved communities, this delay creates a cascade of challenges—from academic setbacks to social isolation. The path to early identification is often blocked by high costs, lack of awareness, and insufficient services, perpetuating a cycle of disadvantage.
